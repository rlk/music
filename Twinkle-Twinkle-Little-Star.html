1. When the blazing sun is gone,
    When he nothing shines upon,
    Then you show your little light,
    Twinkle, twinkle, through the night.
  2. Then the traveller in the dark
    Thanks you for your tiny spark;
    He could not see where to go,
    If you did not twinkle so.
  3. In the dark blue sky you keep,
    And often through my curtains peep,
    For you never shut your eye
    Till the sun is in the sky.
  4. As your bright and tiny spark
    Lights the traveller in the dark,
    Though I know not what you are,
    Twinkle, twinkle, little star.

Each of the verses of the original poem has four lines, but the common arrangement has six. Personal preference dictates how to manage this difference. I find the AB-CD-AB repetition so familiar on the first verse to be nonsensical on later verses. The notated repeat permits the performance of all five verses with a satisfying resolution on the last.
